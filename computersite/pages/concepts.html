Embedded computers:

A fire alarm control pannel, is an example of an embedded computer.

Embedded computers, are tiny computers embedded into products to perform specific functions or tasks for that product.


Mobile devices:

Apple Iphone 11.

Mobile phones, small tablets, and other small personal devices that contain built-in computing or Internet capabilities.


Personal computers:

"Fully functional".... Most of the time...

Fully functioning portable or desktop computers that are designed to be used by a single individual at a time.


Servers:

A server rack containing multiple servers.

Servers are computers that host data and programs available to a small group of users.


Mainframe computers:

A Mainframe.

Mainframe computers are powerful computers used to host a large amount of data and programs available to a wide group of users.

Personal note from Leah: "I felt it was important to mention that mainframes are often no longer needed in most use cases*. as currently more is done with servers, due to their increasing performance abilities, and their far cheaper price per unit. However mainframes still do hold a place in science and medicine, and in other field's that require complex tasks or calculations."

Super (and Quantum) computers:

Supercomputers are extremely powerful computers used for complex computations and processing.
